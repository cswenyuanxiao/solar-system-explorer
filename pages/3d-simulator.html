Learn About the Solar System

Planetary Orbits

All planets orbit the Sun in elliptical paths. The inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) are rocky, while the outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune) are gas giants.

Scale and Distance

The distances between planets are vast. If Earth were the size of a marble, Jupiter would be the size of a basketball, and the Sun would be 109 times larger than Earth.

Time and Motion

Planets move at different speeds. Mercury completes an orbit in 88 Earth days, while Neptune takes 165 Earth years to orbit the Sun once.
