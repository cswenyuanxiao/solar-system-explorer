Mars

The Red Planet - Future Human Destination

The Red Planet

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System. Known as the Red Planet due to iron oxide on its surface, it's the most explored planet beyond Earth.

Basic Facts

  • Type: Terrestrial Planet
  • Distance from Sun: 227.9 million km
  • Diameter: 6,792 km (0.53 × Earth)
  • Mass: 6.39 × 10²³ kg (0.107 × Earth)
  • Surface Temperature: -140°C to 20°C
  • Orbital Period: 687 Earth days

Atmosphere & Climate

  • Atmosphere: Carbon dioxide (95%)
  • Surface Pressure: 0.006 atm (1% of Earth)
  • Dust Storms: Global storms possible
  • Polar Ice Caps: Water and CO₂ ice
  • Wind Speed: Up to 110 km/h
  • Seasonal Changes: Similar to Earth

Surface Features

  • Olympus Mons: Largest volcano (21.9 km)
  • Valles Marineris: Canyon system (4,000 km)
  • Tharsis Bulge: Volcanic plateau
  • Impact Craters: Heavily cratered surface
  • Sand Dunes: Extensive dune fields
  • Ancient Riverbeds: Evidence of water

Moons & Satellites

  • Phobos: Larger moon (22.2 km)
  • Deimos: Smaller moon (12.6 km)
  • Origin: Captured asteroids
  • Orbital Period: 7.7 and 30.3 hours
  • Future: Phobos will crash into Mars
  • Exploration: Potential human base

Exploration History

  • Mariner 4: First flyby (1965)
  • Viking Program: First landers (1976)
  • Pathfinder: Sojourner rover (1997)
  • Spirit & Opportunity: Rovers (2004-2019)
  • Curiosity: Active rover (2012-)
  • Perseverance: Latest rover (2021-)

Future Human Missions

  • SpaceX Starship: Planned missions
  • NASA Artemis: Moon to Mars program
  • Habitat Design: Underground shelters
  • Resource Utilization: ISRU technology
  • Radiation Protection: Shielding required
  • Life Support: Closed-loop systems

Fun Facts

Tallest Mountain

Olympus Mons on Mars is the tallest mountain in the solar system, three times higher than Mount Everest.

Ancient Oceans

Mars once had liquid water on its surface, with evidence of ancient riverbeds and lake beds.

Dust Devils

Mars has frequent dust devils that can reach heights of 8 kilometers and last for hours.

Rusty Surface

Mars appears red because its surface is covered in iron oxide (rust), giving it the nickname "Red Planet."
